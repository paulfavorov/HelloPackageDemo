How it works

Four steps, every package.

A consistent, automated workflow from the moment a driver scans a parcel to the moment a resident walks out with it. No staff intervention required.

Step 01
Driver guidance.

Automatically grant access and train drivers to follow a guided process and log every package as they arrive. 98.7% driver participation — no more "dump and go".

Step 02
Logged & stored.

Multiple indoor kiosks and computer vision track every drop-off in real time. Stored in the secure, access-controlled room with cameras on.

Step 03
Resident alerts.

Pickup instructions sent automatically. 24/7 secure access with a personal code. Resident picks up on their schedule.

Step 04
Automated security monitoring.

Every interaction recorded. Full chain-of-custody. Package volume, resident duration, and room state visible to property in the Dashboard.

Common objections

The three big hurdles.

When prospects pass on HelloPackage, the reason is almost always one of three things. Honest answers on each.

If your property has unused interior square footage — lobbies, leasing back-rooms, ground-floor storage — we convert it in 3 weeks turnkey. If it doesn't, we deliver a modular climate-controlled package room. Avana Copper Springs is the case study: 366 units, no convertible space, we brought the room.

Four tiers — Core through Modular — covering every property profile and budget. Cap-ex from $12K, monthly from $1.70/unit. Op Ex financing available. 755 North switched from a more expensive competitor with no capital budget — and ended under budget on the new contract.

Some operators charge residents, some absorb the cost, some bundle into amenity fees. We've supported all three. Where resident fees apply, the modeled property uplift is ~$480K per 250-unit asset. Where they don't, staff-time savings alone justify the line item.

3-week turnkey from contract for a typical conversion. Modular package rooms ship in a similar window. Portfolios roll out in waves of 5–10 properties at a time, with a named CSM per portfolio.

Lockers solve storage — we run the entire process. They rate LOW on driver management, full service, and provided onsite labor — and MIXED on resident satisfaction. They also can't handle ~25% of packages that need overflow space (oversized parcels, holiday surges). For low-volume properties, lockers may be enough. For real volume, you'll outgrow them.

Offsite is the wrong direction for resident satisfaction — rated POOR in our comparison. Residents want their packages onsite, immediately, at 11pm. Offsite means an extra hop, an extra fee, and a stack of complaints.
